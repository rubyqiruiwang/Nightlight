 The final dataset is an unbalanced panel of 6,423 leader-year observations, covering 1,237 distinct leadership spells across 1,095 leaders in 172 countries over the period
  1992–2023. Nightlight data are drawn from two sources: the Defense Meteorological Satellite Program (DMSP), which accounts for 4,394 observations (68.4%) spanning 1992–2013,  
  and the Visible Infrared Imaging Radiometer Suite (VIIRS), which contributes 2,029 observations (31.6%) for the period 2014–2023. VIIRS values have been calibrated to the DMSP
   scale using a power-function regression estimated on the overlapping period, with an R² of 0.72. The average leadership spell in the sample lasts approximately 5.2 years,    
  with a median of 4 years and a maximum of 32 years. Approximately 5.0% of observations correspond to foreign-born leaders. Geographic coordinates are geocoded to the
  sub-national level: 87.6% of observations carry the highest precision score (geo precision = 1, city-level), indicating strong spatial accuracy in the underlying PLAD dataset.
   The dataset excludes Kosovo and Northern Cyprus due to the absence of corresponding nightlight records, as well as a small number of observations with missing coordinates or
  no matching administrative unit in the GAUL (2015) boundary files.
